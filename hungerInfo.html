What does it mean to be Food Insecure?

The USDA uses the term Food Insecurity to define circumstances where nutritious foods are not always accessible to all members of a household, ensuring a healthy diet and overall well-being. These households may flutuate from Food Insecurity to food secure at any given time. Many of these households must make a choice between housing or medical bills and purchasing nutritious foods.

What are the effects of a nutritiously inadequate diet?

A poor diet may contribute or lead to ailments. Feelings of fatigue, stress, and lack of ability to perform certain tasks are also noted in those who are not eating healthy foods. Children are effected in their growth physically and are also not able to concentrate fully on important tasks like schoolwork.
